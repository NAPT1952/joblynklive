How to Crack a Sales Executive Interview in India: Word-for-Word Scripts That Actually Work

Category: Interview Tips | Non-Tech Jobs India | Sales Careers
Read Time: 7 min | Published on: JobLynk.live

Whether you are a fresh graduate eyeing your first corporate role or a seasoned professional looking to switch companies, landing a Sales Executive position in India requires more than just charm. Recruiters in FMCG, banking, telecom, real estate, and retail are looking for candidates who can sell themselves before they sell a product.

This guide gives you real, word-for-word scripts that land offers, along with the reasoning behind every response. Whether you are interviewing at Hindustan Unilever, HDFC Bank, Airtel, or a growing D2C brand, these scripts are built for the Indian job market.

The 7 Most Common Sales Executive Interview Questions With Scripts

1. Tell me about yourself.

I am [Name], and I have spent the last X years in field sales, specifically in the FMCG or banking segment. In my previous role at [Company], I managed a territory covering a region and consistently achieved over 110 percent of my monthly target. I am now looking for a role where I can grow into a senior sales position.

2. Why do you want to work in sales?

I genuinely enjoy the challenge of persuasion understanding what a customer actually needs and matching it with the right solution. What draws me to sales is the direct feedback loop. You know within days whether your approach is working. I find that accountability energising rather than stressful.

3. Tell me about a time you exceeded a sales target.

In Q3 of last year, our team was behind on targets due to a festive season slowdown. My target for October was 8 lakh rupees. I shifted my strategy to re-engage dormant accounts from the past six months. By the end of the month, I had closed 10.4 lakh rupees, which was 130 percent of target.

4. How do you handle rejection?

I treat rejection as information, not failure. If a prospect says no, I ask why. Understanding the reason helps me refine my pitch. About 20 percent of my closed deals in the last year started as a rejection.

5. Sell me this pen.

Before I tell you about this pen, could I ask how often you write during a typical workday? What frustrates you most about your current pen? Based on what you shared, this pen has quick-dry gel ink that prevents smudging. Would you like to try it?

6. Where do you see yourself in five years?

In five years, I see myself in a regional sales management role, overseeing a team. I want to develop my skills in sales analytics and CRM tools so I can contribute through building a high-performing team.

7. What is your expected salary?

Based on my research into market standards for this role and given my experience, I am looking at something in the range of X to Y per month, with a performance incentive structure. I am flexible and open to understanding the overall compensation package.

Dress the part. Business casuals are minimum. For premium brands or banking roles, go full formal.
Carry a printed resume. Bring at least three copies. This signals preparation and professionalism.
Research the company's products. Know their top products, recent campaigns, and key competitors.
Ask smart closing questions. Ask: What does success look like in the first 90 days?

Final Thoughts

Sales is one of the most democratic careers in India. It rewards performance over pedigree and hustle over heritage. With the right scripts and preparation, you can walk out of your next sales executive interview with an offer in hand.
